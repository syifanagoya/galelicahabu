How did Katie Holmes start her Professional Career?

Katie Holmes’ modeling career began when she entered the International Modelling and Talent Association (IMTA) Competition in New York City in 1996 as a young model. She made her feature film debut in 1997’s The Ice Storm, as Libbets Casey. Both the crowd and the directors loved her performance, and she went on to play a number of more roles in movies.

The next year, she played Rachel Wagner in the film Disturbing Behaviour (1998), for which she was nominated for both the Saturn Award for Best Performance by a Younger Actor and the MTV Movie Award for Best Breakthrough Performance. She received her first significant break and her first lead role in the 1999 movie Teaching Mrs. Tingle.

She received MTV Movie Award nominations for Best Kiss and Teen Choice Award-Choice Chemistry for her portrayal of Leigh Ann Watson in the movie. As Joey Potter, she made her television debut in Dawson’s Creek (1998–2003). She received many nominations for her first television appearance, including the Teen Choice Award for Best TV Actress.

Holmes played Rachel Dawes in Batman Begins (2005), for which she was nominated for both the Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Supporting Actress and the Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress. The 2010 movie The Romantics served as her debut production. Her most recent role was as Rita Carmichael in the 2016 film All We Had, for which she also served as producer and director.
