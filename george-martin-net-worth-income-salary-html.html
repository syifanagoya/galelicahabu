Sir George Henry Martin CBE (born 3 January 1926) is an English record producer, arranger, composer, conductor, audio engineer, and musician who has worked in a variety of fields including music and film. As a result of his substantial participation with each of the Beatles’ original recordings, he is frequently referred to as “the Fifth Beatle.” He is widely regarded as one of the best record producers of all time, having produced 30 number one hit songs in the United Kingdom and 23 number one hits in the United States, among other accomplishments. While at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama from 1947 to 1950, he was influenced by a variety of musical genres, including Cole Porter and Johnny Dankworth. He also studied piano and oboe during his time there. Following graduation, he worked for the BBC’s classical music department for a year before joining EMI as a recording engineer in 1950. Martin was a comic and novelty record producer in the early 1950s, and he collaborated with stars such as Peter Sellers and Spike Milligan, among others. Martin has worked in a variety of fields, including music, cinema, television, and live performance, during the course of his six-decade career. In addition, he has held a number of top executive positions at media firms and is involved in a variety of humanitarian endeavours, including his work with the Prince’s Trust and the Caribbean island of Montserrat, among others. In 1996, he was awarded the title of Knight Bachelor in appreciation of his contributions to the music business and popular culture.
